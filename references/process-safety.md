# Process Safety and Final Review

## Timing

- Finish major CV edits at least 2 days before the deadline.
- Avoid making structural edits on the last day unless the change is urgent.
- Keep dated backups after every major edit so accidental portal corruption can be recovered quickly.

## Portal safety

- Wait for the CV portal/editor page to fully load before editing, previewing, or saving.
- After each meaningful edit, export or download the source/HTML/PDF if the portal allows it.
- If a portal is known to distort bullets, bold text, spacing, or styles, use the browser that is known to be stable for that portal.
- For browser-based editors, keep a local plain-text or HTML backup that can be pasted back if the page loses content.
- Preview after save, not just before save.

## Review workflow

- Get at least one relevant senior, peer, mentor, or domain reviewer to review the CV before submission.
- Ask reviewers to check both content and explainability, not just grammar.
- Print the CV once if recruiters may read physical copies; check font size, whitespace, margins, and bold readability on paper.
- Run a grammar pass with Grammarly or an equivalent spelling/grammar checker.
- Verify proper nouns and official capitalization from reliable sources: programming languages, tools, libraries, companies, competitions, and institutes.

## Truth and interview-readiness

- Be honest. Do not add claims, numbers, tools, or systems you cannot explain under interview pressure.
- Keep only points that the candidate can defend with architecture, code, tradeoffs, metrics, or examples.
- Decide target profiles before final ordering. Tech resumes should prioritize internships, projects, systems, coding, AI/data, and skills over generic leadership filler.
- Remove repeated or lower-level achievements when a stronger achievement proves the same capability.
- Use interview experiences and mock interviews after finalizing the CV so every bullet can be explained clearly.

## Project affiliation

- Mention project affiliation where it helps external recruiters understand context:
  - Competition/project name for competition work
  - Professor/professional/department/institute for supervised work
  - `Self-Project` for independent work
  - GitHub, live demo, blog, or portfolio link when the artifact is presentable
- Do not spend space explaining the organization unless it clarifies the candidate's scope, ownership, or result.
