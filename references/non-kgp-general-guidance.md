# Generalized Peer Guidance

This file captures non-institute-specific resume guidance from peer review notes and campus placement advice. It intentionally removes local names, internal portal jargon, and location-specific assumptions unless the rule generalizes.

## Timing and Review

- Finish major edits at least 2 days before the deadline
- Do not open the final resume for structural edits on the last day unless a critical correction is needed
- Ask relevant seniors, peers, mentors, alumni, or domain professionals to review the resume
- Do not hesitate to request feedback; resume quality is often a deciding factor before interviews
- Carry a printed copy when interviews may involve physical resume review
- Print once before final submission to check font size, margins, density, and bold readability

## Honesty and Interview Readiness

- Be as honest as possible
- Do not fake tools, metrics, rankings, responsibilities, or project ownership
- Include only points you can explain under interview pressure
- Prepare interview explanations for every bullet after the resume is finalized
- Use mock interviews and coding practice to make resume claims defensible

## Bullet Style

- Do not end bullets with full stops
- Put exactly one space after each bullet marker
- Start every bullet with a strong action verb
- Avoid repeating the same action verb across nearby bullets
- Keep each bullet to one idea
- Use STAR/XYZ thinking while keeping the final bullet compact
- Replace adjectives and vague claims with numbers, scope, systems, or results
- Highlight individual contribution instead of describing the organization, society, competition, or summit
- Keep project bullets under a reasonable count; usually 3-6 strong bullets beat many weak ones

## Metrics and Impact

- Add accurate numbers wherever possible
- Use model metrics, latency, throughput, data volume, users, records, cost, revenue, rank, team size, code size, deployment count, endpoint count, or time saved
- Do not invent numbers
- If exact impact is unavailable, use verifiable scale from source material such as repos, integrations, services, files, releases, or records
- Prefer `Reduced latency from 4s to 843ms` over `Made the system faster`
- Prefer `Ranked 3/1,200` over `Performed well`

## Formatting

- Keep formatting consistent across all sections
- Keep body font at 10pt or 11pt; avoid 9pt or smaller
- Keep bold text slightly restrained so it does not overpower body text
- Put one space after commas and colons, never before them
- Put spaces around standalone hyphens and pipe characters
- Do not put spaces around slashes
- Do not put spaces inside brackets
- Avoid double or triple spaces
- Use full years such as `2026`, not `'26`
- Use consistent month-year dates such as `Apr 2021 - Jul 2021`
- Use `Present` with a capital `P`
- For platforms that explicitly prefer numeric ATS dates, use `04/2021 - 07/2021`

## Content Ordering

- Put the strongest and most relevant content in the first half
- Decide the target company/profile family before ordering sections
- For technical roles, prioritize experience, projects, skills, systems, and measurable engineering work
- Reduce generic positions of responsibility when they crowd out technical content
- Do not repeat lower-level achievements when a higher-level achievement proves the same point
- Split Positions and Achievements only when both have strong, role-relevant evidence

## Jargon and External Readability

- Avoid local acronyms, institute-specific halls/societies, and unexplained internal terms
- Expand or translate terms so external recruiters understand them
- Verify official names and capitalization for programming languages, libraries, companies, tools, competitions, and products
- Use correct alumni terms: alumnus, alumna, alumni, or alum depending on context

## Projects

- Mention project affiliation when it clarifies credibility
- Use `Self-Project` for independent work
- Mention professor, lab, department, company, or competition context when relevant
- Add GitHub, live demo, blog, paper, or product links when they are presentable
- Ensure linked GitHub repositories have useful README files before adding them

## Portal and Browser Safety

- Wait for browser-based resume editors to fully load before editing or previewing
- Download/export an HTML or source backup after each meaningful edit
- Preview after saving because portals may distort bullets, bold text, spacing, or content
- Keep local backups so content can be restored if the portal loses formatting
- Use a stable browser for the target portal if one browser is known to corrupt styles

## Final Review

- Run grammar and spelling checks
- Check extracted PDF text for ordering and missing content
- Check links manually
- Check phone/email visibility
- Check whether every bullet can be defended in an interview
- Check that the final document is readable when printed
